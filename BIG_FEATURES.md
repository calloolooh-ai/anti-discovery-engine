# Big Features — Hackathon Judge Impressors

Judging weights: Originality 35% · Technical Depth 25% · Vision 20% · Feasibility 10% · Demo 10%
The features that win aren't more polish — they're moments that make judges put down their phones.

---

## The Features

### 1. Scientific Breakthrough Predictor
Run the engine on historical corpora (1990, 2000, 2005) and show it would have predicted **multiple** Nobel Prize-winning discoveries before they happened. Not just the epidemiology example — show 5-6 cases. Penicillin. DNA. Deep learning. CRISPR. Each one: "Gap visible in [year]. Discovery made in [year+N]. Engine would have found it." Proof by historical validation at scale. Single most powerful demo possible.

### 2. The Cascade Map
When you close a gap, other gaps automatically become closable downstream. Show a **ripple effect**: click a gap, watch the graph animate which other gaps this discovery would unlock. Like a domino chain through the knowledge graph. "Closing this one gap enables these 7 other discoveries." Technically: betweenness centrality + reachability. Buildable in a day.

### 3. Time-Lapse of Science
Animate the knowledge graph evolving from 1980 → today. Watch fields emerge, connect, fragment. Watch gaps open and close. A 60-second animation of scientific progress. Judges would remember this for weeks. Reframes the entire product: not a search tool — a telescope pointed at the future.

### 4. The Orphaned Insight
Concepts published once, cited heavily for a few years, then completely abandoned. Someone found something important — nobody followed up. Highest-value gaps because the intellectual work is partially done. Surface the top 10 "forgotten breakthroughs." Frame: *"Science's lost luggage."*

### 5. Gap Difficulty Score
Not just "is there a gap" but "how hard to close it." Score each gap on: estimated years, required disciplines, required equipment, team size, estimated funding. Show the "easy wins" — high leverage, low difficulty. Makes the tool actionable, not just observational.

### 6. The Serendipity Engine
Find gaps closable by **accident** — where a researcher in field A doing normal work would stumble onto the answer to a question in field B without knowing it. "A materials scientist running routine tests on graphene would accidentally answer this open question in neuroscience." Genuinely zero-to-one framing no other tool does.

### 7. Institutional Blind Spot Detector
Show which high-leverage gaps are NOT being explored because no major institution has claimed the territory. Cross-reference gaps with affiliation data. The map of what science is *avoiding* — unfundable, unfashionable, or falling between departments. Frame: *"The gaps everyone agrees matter but nobody is studying."*

### 8. "What If" Simulator
Let users manually close a gap by drawing an edge — then watch the graph re-simulate. What does the knowledge map look like if this discovery happened in 1990? In 2030? What gaps does it open? Interactive counterfactual history of science. Memorable, unique, genuinely fun to demo.

### 9. The Antimatter Query
For every established finding, surface its untested logical inverse. *"We know X causes Y. Has anyone tested whether Y causes X? 0 papers over 40 years."* The inverse of discoveries is systematically unstudied. A completely novel query type no tool attempts.

### 10. Live Gap Monitoring
Public page that updates daily as new arxiv papers come in — showing which gaps are narrowing in real time. A gap goes from "0 papers" to "2 papers": show the moment a gap starts closing. Frame: *"A live seismograph for scientific progress."*
