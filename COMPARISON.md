## Verdict: Anti-Discovery Engine wins — but narrowly

**Anti-Discovery Engine is the stronger submission for this hackathon.**

Here's why:

### ADE's advantages
1. **Demo is viscerally impressive.** A visual knowledge graph with highlighted unexplored gaps and AI-generated research questions is something a judge can immediately understand and feel the weight of. No explanation needed.

2. **Historical validation is killer.** Showing that the system would have found the epidemiology/network-science gap before 2020, or the deep learning gap before 2012, is a concrete proof-of-vision that's hard to argue with.

3. **Paper writes itself.** The problem (question selection bias in science), the insight (knowledge is a graph, gaps are computable), and the implications (compress civilizational discovery) are crisp and airtight.

4. **More buildable in one day.** Semantic Scholar API is public. NetworkX is mature. You can have a working, impressive demo by afternoon.

### FCP's advantages
1. **Higher concept ceiling.** "Context-free communication for any intelligence" is a more radical zero-to-one claim. If a judge deeply engages with it, it scores higher on originality.

2. **Cross-domain ambition.** Solves AI alignment, SETI, and post-singularity communication simultaneously. That breadth is genuinely impressive.

3. **No one else will submit this.** It's a more unusual idea — lower risk of collision with other teams.

### Why ADE wins anyway
This hackathon is **one day.** The prototype is 10% of judging but it still matters — and more importantly, a weak or confusing demo hurts your paper and presentation scores too because judges lose confidence. ADE lets you build a strong, legible demo that reinforces the paper. FCP risks having a demo that requires a 10-minute explanation before judges understand what they're looking at.

---

## Recommendation

**Build the Anti-Discovery Engine.**

If you have strong math/philosophy background and can write the FCP paper at a level that makes judges feel like they're reading something from the future, FCP has higher upside. But for a one-day hackathon where demo legibility matters, ADE is the safer moonshot.

---

## Hybrid Option

The two ideas are not mutually exclusive at the conceptual level.

The Anti-Discovery Engine finds the gaps in human knowledge.
The First Contact Protocol is itself a gap the Anti-Discovery Engine might identify — a missing bridge between information theory, cognitive science, and communication theory.

You could frame ADE as the meta-tool, and FCP as a demonstration of the kind of question it would surface. One system, two papers worth of depth.
